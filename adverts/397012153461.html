Pippy Oak is a highly decorative variant of European Oak, characterized by dense clusters of small knots and swirls that resemble cat's paw prints, often called "cats paw oak". These patterns are caused by dormant buds beneath the bark, which become encapsulated in the wood as the tree adapts to potential crown damage, creating a rustic, textured, and unique appearance highly sought after for high-end furniture, joinery, and decorative pieces.
